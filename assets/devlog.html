🐿️ Chris’s Devlog #1: Why I Made Ultimate Cat Clicker

“Some people dream of success. I dream of cats clicking coins while I eat cold fries.”

So here’s the story:
It was 3AM. I had a half-finished McChicken, a blank HTML file, and a brain full of chaos. I could’ve gone to bed. I could’ve done literally anything else. But no—I decided to make a game where cats click things. And thus, Ultimate Cat Clicker was born.

🧪 What Started as a Joke…

  • I thought: “What if cats ran an empire powered by clicks?”
  • I wanted something dumb, fast, and weirdly satisfying.
  • I didn’t expect it to spiral into a full-blown kingdom with upgrades, skins, and lore.

🐾 What I Learned

  • Clickers are addictive. Even when you’re the one building them.
  • Pixel art is pain. Especially when animating tails and paws.
  • Cats are perfect protagonists. They do nothing and still win.
  • I code best under sleep deprivation. Not recommended, but effective.

🔥 The Chaos Behind the Code

  • Most of UCC was built while listening to vaporwave remixes of McDonald’s commercials.
  • The fishing edition? That was a fever dream turned feature.
  • Every bug I fixed introduced two new ones. I call that “feature stacking.”

🐿️ Final Thoughts from Chris

I’m not a professional. I’m a squirrel in a flaming McDonald’s hat trying to survive the internet.
If you’re here for polished code and clean UI, you’re in the wrong forest.
But if you like cats, chaos, and cursed creativity—welcome to the treehouse.

—Dexter the Squirrel 🐿️🔥
